# Privacy Policy

(pursuant to Article 13 of Regulation (EU) 2016/679 - GDPR)

## 1. Data Controller

The Data Controller is:

- Davide Cuni
- Email: `info@typotek.space`

---

## 2. Types of data processed

### 2.1 Data voluntarily provided by the user

Through the contact form available on the website, the following data may be collected:

**Contact form**
- name
- email address
- message

The website does not include a newsletter, marketing subscription form, quote calculator, commercial request workflow, or optional marketing consent.

The website does not store contact form submissions in an internal application database. Depending on the technical configuration, the data entered in the contact form may be transmitted by email or processed by technical service providers solely for the purpose of delivering the message to the Data Controller and allowing a reply.

### 2.2 Technical and browsing data

During browsing, the website and the technical systems used to operate it may process technical data whose transmission is implicit in the use of Internet protocols and IT systems, such as:

- IP address
- date and time of the request
- browser, operating system, and device information
- requested URLs
- server response codes
- technical logs related to security and website operation

These data are processed for technical, security, diagnostic, and maintenance purposes.

### 2.3 Privacy/cookie preferences and third-party services

The website stores a necessary first-party technical cookie (`dc_consent`) to remember the user's privacy/cookie preferences.

Only with the user's consent, the website may activate optional third-party services that may process technical and personal data, use cookies or similar identifiers, and receive information relating to the user's device and browsing activity, including:

- Google Analytics 4, if configured by the Data Controller
- Cloudflare Turnstile, for third-party anti-spam verification of the contact form

## 3. Purposes of processing and legal bases

### 3.1 Handling messages sent by the user

Purpose: to receive and reply to messages sent through the contact form or other contact details published on the website.  
Legal basis: Article 6(1)(f) GDPR, the legitimate interest of the Data Controller in responding to messages voluntarily sent by users. Where the message concerns a specific request that may require pre-contractual steps, the legal basis may also be Article 6(1)(b) GDPR.

### 3.2 Website operation, security, and abuse prevention

Purpose: to ensure the proper functioning of the website, protect the infrastructure, prevent spam, abusive access, or fraudulent use, and manage technical logs.  
Legal basis: Article 6(1)(f) GDPR, the legitimate interest of the Data Controller in maintaining the security and reliability of the website and its services.

### 3.3 Privacy/cookie preference management and activation of optional services

Purpose: to store and respect the user's privacy/cookie choices and, only where authorized, activate optional third-party services for analytics and third-party anti-spam verification.  
Legal basis:

- Article 6(1)(f) GDPR for storing technical preferences necessary to manage the cookie banner
- Article 6(1)(a) GDPR for activating optional services subject to consent, such as Google Analytics 4 and Cloudflare Turnstile

---

## 4. Nature of data provision

Providing data through the contact form is voluntary. However, failure to provide the required fields may prevent the Data Controller from receiving or replying to the user's message.

Acknowledgement of the Privacy Policy may be required before submitting the contact form, as it confirms that the user has received information about the processing of personal data.

Consent to optional analytics services is voluntary. If consent is not provided, Google Analytics 4 is not loaded.

Consent to third-party anti-spam verification is voluntary. If consent is not provided, Cloudflare Turnstile is not loaded and the contact form cannot be submitted through the website. The user may still contact the Data Controller through the published email address.

---

## 5. Processing methods

Processing is carried out using electronic and telematic tools, in accordance with the principles of lawfulness, fairness, transparency, relevance, and data minimization.

Appropriate technical and organizational measures are adopted to protect personal data against unauthorized access, loss, disclosure, alteration, or unlawful use.

---

## 6. Data retention

Personal data are retained only for the time strictly necessary to achieve the purposes described above and, in particular:

- contact form submissions: not stored in the website's internal application database; if received by email, they are retained for the time necessary to handle the request and any related follow-up and, in any case, normally no longer than 12 months from the last relevant contact, unless further retention is required by law or to protect the Data Controller's rights
- technical and security logs: normally no longer than 14 days, unless further retention is necessary in case of anomalies, incidents, or requests from competent authorities
- privacy/cookie preferences stored on the user's device: up to 12 months or until earlier deletion by the user; the banner may be shown again if the policy is updated or relevant settings change
- analytics cookies and related identifiers: managed by Google according to the configured settings and browser limitations; by default, Google Analytics 4 cookies may persist for up to 24 months
- analytics reports and event data: retained according to the retention settings configured in Google Analytics, where the service is activated with the user's consent

Personal data may be processed by:

Such transfers are carried out in compliance with Articles 44 et seq. of the GDPR, on the basis of the legal instruments provided by applicable law, such as European Commission adequacy decisions, including the EU-US Data Privacy Framework where applicable, Standard Contractual Clauses, or other applicable transfer mechanisms adopted by the relevant provider.

## 9. Rights of the data subject

The data subject may exercise, where applicable, the rights provided by Articles 15-22 GDPR, including the right to:

- obtain confirmation as to whether personal data concerning them are being processed
- access their personal data
- request rectification or updating
- request erasure
- request restriction of processing
- object to processing in the cases provided by law
- receive the data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format, where applicable
- withdraw consent at any time, without affecting the lawfulness of processing based on consent before withdrawal

Requests may be sent to: `info@typotek.space`

The data subject also has the right to lodge a complaint with the competent supervisory authority. In Italy, this is the **Garante per la protezione dei dati personali**.

The website does not carry out automated decision-making producing legal effects or similarly significant effects on the data subject pursuant to Article 22 GDPR.
