## Overview

**Animazioni e Spettacoli** is an artist association active in northern Italy, offering entertainment, performances, and event services for private parties, ceremonies, and public events.

This project involved redesigning the website from the ground up, covering structure, visual design, development, and a significant portion of the copywriting. The biggest challenge was not just making the homepage look nicer. It was taking a huge catalogue of services and turning it into something a real person could browse without getting lost halfway through.

## Context

The original website had grown into a large collection of pages with overlapping content and inconsistent messaging. It included around 40 pages and, in some cases, non-original text that needed to be replaced.

That created two clear problems:

- The navigation was difficult to follow
- The content quality needed to be improved for credibility and search visibility

That is not exactly a strong foundation for usability or SEO. Rather than redesigning the existing structure page by page, I treated the project as a broader content and UX reorganization.

## Approach

The first step was simplifying the information architecture. I reduced the number of pages from **40 to 26**, grouping similar offers into clearer categories such as artists, shows, and services. Related activities were consolidated into thematic pages so users could browse more naturally instead of bouncing between near-duplicate pages.

::DcpImg{src="/images/blog/animaz_services.png" alt="Entertainment for Weddings and Ceremonies"}
::

The second major part of the work was copywriting. Since there was no dedicated writer on the project, I rewrote the content myself, researching each performer and service to produce original text tailored to the association's offer. In total, the project resulted in roughly **7,000 words** across **32 services**.

That part took much longer than I naively expected when I first said yes to it, especially while I was still studying, but it ended up being one of the most useful writing exercises I had done up to that point.

On the development side, I built the website in WordPress using a custom child theme based on OceanWP and extended WPBakery with ad-hoc components to support the required layouts and content blocks. Part of that work also helped me build reusable tools I could carry into later projects instead of reinventing the wheel every time.

## Result

The final website became a much clearer and more content-rich platform for the association. It presents a wide range of services in a more structured way, reduces friction in navigation, and gives potential clients a better understanding of what the organization offers before reaching out.

Visually, the result remained colorful and expressive, but the real improvement was structural: visitors could understand the offer without fighting the navigation.

It was also one of the first projects where design, development, content strategy, and copywriting all had to work together at the same time. That made it messy in places, but also genuinely formative.

### Key Features

- Reworked site structure with fewer, more focused pages
- Service categories for artists, shows, and event solutions
- Original copywriting for dozens of service pages
- Contact-driven website flow for inquiries

### Tech Stack

- WordPress
- WPBakery Page Builder
- Custom shortcodes plugin
- Custom child theme based on OceanWP
